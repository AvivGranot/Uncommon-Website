Uncommon People in a Common World

"If you would not be forgotten, as soon as you are dead and rotten, either write things worth reading, or do things worth writing"

Benjamin Franklin

In 2022, I experienced my most significant personal and professional growth, yet I felt it didn't mean a thing to the greater world. More importantly, it didn't create true value at scale. As I dived deeper into the concept of Commonness vs. Uncommonness, I realized something: while we perceive our lives as unique, we are actually far more common than uncommon. We are rarely taught to think differently or to expand our purpose beyond what we believe is possible. How can we aspire to be the next 'Da Vinci,' or at least aim for that kind of legacy? And why must we refuse to compromise on mediocrity, especially when our environment encourages it? After many conversations with friends, mentors, and peers, I was encouraged to finally put these thoughts into words. I hope they weren't wrong. Enjoy the ride!
